# Digital Forensics

## About

Digital Forensics is the process of identifying, collecting, preserving, analyzing, and presenting digital evidence from computers, mobile devices, networks, and other digital systems. It is used to investigate cybercrimes, security incidents, and legal cases while ensuring the integrity of the evidence.

Digital forensics plays an important role in law enforcement, cybersecurity, incident response, and corporate investigations.

---

# What is Digital Forensics?

Digital Forensics is a branch of forensic science that focuses on recovering and investigating data stored in digital devices.

Its main objective is to collect evidence in a manner that is legally acceptable and scientifically reliable.

---

# Objectives of Digital Forensics

- Identify Digital Evidence
- Preserve Evidence
- Recover Deleted Data
- Analyze Security Incidents
- Determine Attack Methods
- Support Legal Proceedings
- Prevent Future Incidents

---

# Digital Forensics Process

## 1. Identification

Locate potential sources of digital evidence.

Examples

- Computers
- Mobile Phones
- Hard Drives
- USB Devices
- Servers
- Cloud Storage

---

## 2. Preservation

Protect evidence from alteration or damage.

Activities

- Create Disk Images
- Use Write Blockers
- Secure Storage
- Document Collection Process

---

## 3. Collection

Collect digital evidence using approved forensic methods.

Examples

- Hard Disk Imaging
- Memory Capture
- Log Collection
- Network Traffic Capture

---

## 4. Examination

Recover and organize relevant data.

Activities

- Recover Deleted Files
- Extract Metadata
- Search Keywords
- Identify Hidden Files

---

## 5. Analysis

Analyze collected evidence to determine what happened.

Questions Answered

- Who performed the activity?
- What happened?
- When did it occur?
- Where did it happen?
- How was it performed?

---

## 6. Reporting

Prepare a detailed report containing findings and evidence.

A forensic report should be:

- Accurate
- Complete
- Clear
- Objective
- Legally Defensible

# Types of Digital Forensics

## Computer Forensics

Investigates desktops, laptops, and storage devices.

---

## Mobile Forensics

Analyzes smartphones, tablets, SIM cards, and memory cards.

---

## Network Forensics

Examines network traffic to detect attacks and unauthorized activities.

---

## Memory Forensics

Analyzes RAM to identify running processes, malware, encryption keys, and active network connections.

---

## Cloud Forensics

Investigates cloud environments such as AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

---

## Database Forensics

Examines databases, logs, and transactions for evidence of unauthorized activity.

# Chain of Custody

Chain of Custody documents every person who handled digital evidence from collection to presentation in court.

Importance

- Maintains Evidence Integrity
- Prevents Tampering
- Supports Legal Proceedings

---

# Forensic Imaging

A forensic image is an exact bit-by-bit copy of a storage device.

Benefits

- Preserves Original Evidence
- Enables Safe Analysis
- Prevents Data Modification

---

# Hashing

Hash values verify that evidence has not changed.

Common Hash Algorithms

- MD5
- SHA-1
- SHA-256

If the hash value remains the same before and after analysis, the evidence integrity is maintained.

# Real-Life Example

A company experiences a data breach.

The digital forensics team creates a forensic image of the affected server, collects system logs, analyzes user activity, and identifies that an attacker gained access through a compromised administrator account.

The findings are documented and used to strengthen the organization's security controls.
