# Cyber Security Glossary

## About

This glossary contains commonly used cyber security terms along with their definitions. It serves as a quick reference for students, beginners, and professionals preparing for interviews, certifications, or practical work.

---

# A

## Access Control

A security mechanism that determines who can access specific resources and what actions they are allowed to perform.

---

## AES (Advanced Encryption Standard)

A widely used symmetric encryption algorithm for protecting sensitive data.

---

## Antivirus

Software that detects, prevents, and removes malicious software from computer systems.

---

## Authentication

The process of verifying the identity of a user, device, or application.

---

## Authorization

The process of determining what an authenticated user is allowed to access or perform.

---

# B

## Backup

A copy of important data that can be restored in case of data loss or disaster.

---

## Botnet

A group of infected computers controlled remotely by an attacker.

---

## Brute Force Attack

An attack where multiple password combinations are tried until the correct one is found.

---

# C

## CIA Triad

The three core principles of information security:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

---

## Cipher

An algorithm used to encrypt or decrypt data.

---

## Cryptography

The science of securing information using encryption techniques.

---

## Cyber Attack

An attempt to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems or networks.

---

## Cyber Security

The practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from cyber threats.

---

# D

## Data Breach

Unauthorized access to confidential or sensitive information.

---

## Decryption

The process of converting encrypted data back into its original readable form.

---

## Digital Forensics

The investigation and analysis of digital evidence for legal or security purposes.

---

## DoS (Denial of Service)

An attack that makes a system or service unavailable to legitimate users.

---

## DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)

A DoS attack launched from multiple compromised systems simultaneously.

---

# E

## Encryption

The process of converting readable data into an unreadable format to protect confidentiality.

---

## Ethical Hacking

Authorized security testing performed to identify vulnerabilities before attackers exploit them.

---

## Exploit

A method or code used to take advantage of a software vulnerability.

---

# F

## Firewall

A security system that monitors and filters incoming and outgoing network traffic.

---

## Forensics

The collection, preservation, analysis, and presentation of digital evidence.

---

# G

## Gray Hat Hacker

A hacker who may access systems without permission but generally does not intend to cause harm.

---

# H

## Hacker

A person with technical knowledge who explores or tests computer systems. Depending on authorization and intent, hackers may be ethical or malicious.

---

## Hash Function

A mathematical function that converts data into a fixed-length value used for integrity verification.

---

## Honeypot

A decoy system designed to attract attackers and study their behavior.

---

## HTTPS

A secure version of HTTP that uses TLS encryption.

---

# I

## IDS (Intrusion Detection System)

A security system that detects suspicious or malicious activities.

---

## IPS (Intrusion Prevention System)

A security system that detects and blocks malicious activities automatically.

---

## Incident Response

A structured process for detecting, containing, eradicating, and recovering from security incidents.

---

## Integrity

Ensuring that data remains accurate and unaltered.

---

## IP Address

A unique numerical address assigned to a device on a network.

---

# M

## Malware

Malicious software designed to damage or disrupt systems.

Examples

- Virus
- Worm
- Trojan
- Spyware
- Ransomware

---

## MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)

An authentication method requiring two or more verification factors.

---

# N

## Network Security

The protection of computer networks from unauthorized access and cyber attacks.

---

## Nmap

A network scanning tool used to discover hosts and services.

---

# P

## Packet

A small unit of data transmitted across a network.

---

## Password Manager

Software that securely stores and manages passwords.

---

## Patch

A software update that fixes bugs or security vulnerabilities.

---

## Penetration Testing

An authorized security assessment that simulates attacks to identify vulnerabilities.

---

## Phishing

A social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information.

---

## Port

A logical communication endpoint used by network services.

---

# R

## Ransomware

Malware that encrypts files and demands payment for their recovery.

---

## Risk

The likelihood and impact of a security threat exploiting a vulnerability.

---

## Rootkit

Malware designed to hide itself and provide unauthorized privileged access.

---

# S

## Sandbox

An isolated environment used to safely test programs or analyze suspicious files.

---

## SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)

A platform that collects, analyzes, and correlates security logs.

---

## Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation used to trick people into revealing confidential information.

---

## Spam

Unwanted bulk email messages, often used for advertising or phishing.

---

## Spyware

Malware that secretly monitors user activity and collects information.

---

## SQL Injection (SQLi)

A web application attack that injects malicious SQL commands into database queries.

---

## Symmetric Encryption

Encryption that uses the same key for both encryption and decryption.

---

# T

## Threat

Anything capable of exploiting a vulnerability and causing harm.

---

## Trojan Horse

Malware disguised as legitimate software.

---

## TLS (Transport Layer Security)

A protocol that secures communication over networks.

---

# U

## URL

Uniform Resource Locator used to identify the address of a resource on the internet.

---

# V

## Virus

Malware that attaches itself to legitimate files and spreads when executed.

---

## VPN (Virtual Private Network)

A secure encrypted connection over a public network.

---

## Vulnerability

A weakness in a system that can be exploited by attackers.

---

# W

## WAF (Web Application Firewall)

A firewall designed to protect web applications from common attacks.

---

## White Hat Hacker

An authorized security professional who identifies and reports vulnerabilities.

---

## Wireshark

A network protocol analyzer used to capture and inspect network traffic.

---

## Worm

A self-replicating malware that spreads across networks without user interaction.

---

# X

## XSS (Cross-Site Scripting)

A vulnerability that allows attackers to inject malicious scripts into web pages viewed by other users.

---

# Zero-Day

A security vulnerability that is unknown to the software vendor and has no available patch at the time it is discovered or exploited.

# Summary
