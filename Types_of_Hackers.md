# Types of Hackers

## About

Hackers are individuals who use their technical knowledge to identify, exploit, or secure computer systems and networks. Not all hackers are criminals. Some work legally to improve security, while others perform illegal activities for personal, financial, or political gain.

---

# What is a Hacker?

A Hacker is a person with advanced knowledge of computers, networks, operating systems, and security who can discover vulnerabilities in systems.

Hackers can be:

- Ethical
- Malicious
- Government-Sponsored
- Security Researchers

---

# Types of Hackers

## 1. White Hat Hacker

### Definition

White Hat Hackers are authorized security professionals who legally test systems to identify vulnerabilities before attackers exploit them.

### Also Known As

- Ethical Hacker
- Security Researcher

### Responsibilities

- Perform Penetration Testing
- Conduct Security Audits
- Report Vulnerabilities
- Improve System Security

### Examples

- Security Consultants
- Penetration Testers
- Bug Bounty Hunters

---

# 2. Black Hat Hacker

### Definition

Black Hat Hackers gain unauthorized access to systems for illegal or malicious purposes.

### Objectives

- Steal Data
- Install Malware
- Demand Ransom
- Financial Fraud
- Identity Theft

### Examples

- Ransomware Groups
- Financial Fraudsters
- Cyber Criminals

---

# 3. Grey Hat Hacker

### Definition

Grey Hat Hackers access systems without permission but usually do not intend to cause harm.

They may report discovered vulnerabilities after finding them.

### Characteristics

- No Prior Permission
- May Disclose Security Flaws
- Actions Can Still Be Illegal

---

# 4. Script Kiddie

### Definition

A Script Kiddie has limited technical knowledge and relies on pre-built hacking tools created by others.

### Characteristics

- Beginner
- Uses Ready-Made Tools
- Limited Understanding
- Often Targets Easy Victims

---

# 5. Hacktivist

### Definition

Hacktivists perform cyber attacks to promote political, social, or ideological causes.

### Activities

- Website Defacement
- Data Leaks
- DDoS Attacks
- Public Awareness Campaigns

---

# 6. State-Sponsored Hacker

### Definition

State-Sponsored Hackers work on behalf of a government or intelligence agency.

### Objectives

- Cyber Espionage
- National Security
- Intelligence Gathering
- Critical Infrastructure Attacks

---

# 7. Insider Threat

### Definition

An Insider Threat is an employee, contractor, or trusted individual who misuses authorized access.

### Examples

- Data Theft
- Information Leakage
- Unauthorized Access
- Sabotage

---

# Comparison Table

| Type | Permission | Purpose | Legal Status |
|------|------------|---------|--------------|
| White Hat | Yes | Improve Security | Legal |
| Black Hat | No | Personal Gain | Illegal |
| Grey Hat | No | Find Vulnerabilities | Usually Illegal |

# Ethical Hacking

Ethical Hacking is the authorized process of identifying security vulnerabilities in systems before attackers exploit them.

Ethical Hackers always:

- Obtain Written Permission
- Follow Legal Procedures
- Report Findings
- Maintain Confidentiality

---

# Phases of Ethical Hacking

## 1. Reconnaissance

Collect information about the target.

Examples

- WHOIS Lookup
- Google Dorking
- DNS Enumeration

---

## 2. Scanning

Identify open ports, services, and vulnerabilities.

Tools

- Nmap
- Nessus

---

## 3. Gaining Access

Attempt to exploit identified vulnerabilities.

Examples

- SQL Injection
- Password Attacks
- Exploitation Frameworks

---

## 4. Maintaining Access

Determine whether persistent access can be achieved (only in authorized testing).

---

## 5. Covering Tracks

Malicious attackers often hide evidence of their activities.

Ethical hackers **do not** erase logs or hide their actions unless specifically required as part of an authorized simulation and agreed upon in the engagement scope.

# Real-Life Example

A company hires an Ethical Hacker before launching a new website.

The Ethical Hacker:

- Finds Security Weaknesses
- Reports Vulnerabilities
- Suggests Fixes

The company fixes the issues before attackers can exploit them.
