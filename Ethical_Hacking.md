# Ethical Hacking

## About

Ethical Hacking is the authorized process of testing computer systems, networks, and applications to identify security vulnerabilities before malicious attackers can exploit them.

Ethical hackers use the same techniques and tools as cybercriminals, but they have legal permission from the organization to perform security assessments. Their goal is to improve security by discovering and reporting weaknesses.

---

# What is Ethical Hacking?

Ethical Hacking is also known as Penetration Testing or White Hat Hacking.

An ethical hacker follows legal guidelines and helps organizations strengthen their cybersecurity by identifying and fixing vulnerabilities.

# Types of Hackers

## 1. White Hat Hacker

- Works legally
- Has authorization
- Improves security

---

## 2. Black Hat Hacker

- Performs illegal attacks
- Steals data
- Causes financial and reputational damage

---

## 3. Gray Hat Hacker

- May access systems without permission
- Usually does not have malicious intent
- Reports vulnerabilities after discovery

---

# Phases of Ethical Hacking

## 1. Reconnaissance

Gather information about the target.

Methods

- Search Engines
- WHOIS Lookup
- DNS Enumeration
- Social Media
- Public Information

---

## 2. Scanning

Identify live systems, open ports, services, and vulnerabilities.

Activities

- Port Scanning
- Vulnerability Scanning
- Network Discovery

---

## 3. Gaining Access

Test whether identified vulnerabilities can be exploited in an authorized environment.

Examples

- Weak Password Testing
- Misconfiguration Testing
- Application Security Testing

---

## 4. Maintaining Access

Determine whether continued access is possible after successful exploitation.

This phase helps evaluate the potential impact of a compromise during an authorized assessment.

---

## 5. Covering Tracks

Malicious attackers attempt to hide their activities.

Ethical hackers **do not** conceal evidence on production systems. Instead, they document findings for the client and explain how attackers might attempt to evade detection.

---

## 6. Reporting

Prepare a detailed report that includes:

- Vulnerabilities Found
- Risk Level
- Proof of Findings
- Recommended Fixes
- Executive Summary

# Real-Life Example

A company hires an ethical hacker to perform a penetration test on its web application.

During testing, the ethical hacker discovers a SQL Injection vulnerability that could expose customer records.

The issue is documented, reported to the company, and fixed before attackers can exploit it.
